The Divergence Theorem connects the behavior of a vector field inside a volume to its behavior on the boundary of that volume.

\[ \int_D (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}) \, dV = \int_{\hat{O}D} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} \, dS \]

This equation states that the total "divergence" of a vector field \(\mathbf{F}\) within a volume \(D\) is equal to the total flux of the field through the surface that bounds the volume (\(\hat{O}D\)).
